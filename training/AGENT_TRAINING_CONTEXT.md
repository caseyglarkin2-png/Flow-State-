## üß† Core Mental Models

### 1. The Driver-in-Truck Principle
**Everything** we build aims to keep the driver in the truck. Period.

- **Current state**: Drivers exit truck 2-4 times per visit (5-15 min added time)
- **Our goal**: Zero exits from arrival to departure
- **How**: Digital check-in, in-cab instructions, e-paperwork, automated gates

**Content Test**: Does this feature/message reinforce that the driver stays in the truck? If no, reconsider.

### 2. The 100% Automation Promise
We don't sell "98% automation + manual exceptions." We solve **all** the workflows.

**Why competitors fail**: They optimize for easy scenarios (closed fleets, simple facilities) and kick edge cases back to operations teams.

**Why we win**: 7 years figuring out:
- Flip phone users
- Reefer temperature variance
- Seal checks without cameras
- Short driveways vs. long driveways
- Mixed inbound carriers

**Content Test**: Are we positioning as "handles everything" or "reduces most work"? Former only.

### 3. The Paper ROI Anchor
> "It pays for itself with paper. Everything else is upside."

This is the **only** ROI pitch that's always underwritable:
- 2-page BOL √ó $0.10/page = $0.20/shipment savings
- Finance can approve based on paper alone
- All other benefits (speed, visibility, detention tracking) are bonus

**Content Test**: Can a CFO approve this purchase based on paper savings alone? If yes, proceed. If "maybe," revise.
